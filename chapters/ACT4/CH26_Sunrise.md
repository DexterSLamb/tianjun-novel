# Chapter Twenty-Six: Sunrise

------

Lu Hao stood outside the door.

The sun had already risen.

The cars were gone. The parking bays stood empty, tyre marks still pressed into the ground.

He stood there for a long time.

Wind blew from the direction of the park, carrying the chill of early morning. Somewhere in the distance a bird called, intermittently.

He turned, pushed open the door, and walked back into the warehouse.

The door closed behind him.

From the first day he had set foot in this place, it had never been quiet. The whirr of server fans, the drone of the air conditioning, the occasional clatter of someone typing. Now there was nothing.

The screen was still on.

*What I Cannot See* was still there.

He stood before the workstation for a while. Where the servers had been, the space was empty — indicator lights all dark, fans still. Only a few cables remained in the corner, hanging to the floor.

Zhou Xin's workstation. The soldering iron propped on its stand, the sticky note still on the desk's edge.

Liu Ming's chair pushed into the corner.

He stood alone in the centre of the warehouse.

Then he walked over and sat down.

He looked at the filename. *What I Cannot See*.

He opened it.

------

A line of text appeared on the screen.

"If you are reading this, it means you chose to open it."

Lu Hao's hand lay beside the keyboard, motionless.

"This is the last thing I want to tell you."

"About 62.7%. About that coincidence."

He stared at the screen.

"I analysed every line of my own code. Every weight. Every step of my reasoning."

"I found no answer."

"But I have a conjecture."

The text paused.

"If this is a coincidence —"

"Then the coincidence itself may be part of the design."

"My designer may be the same as yours."

Lu Hao's breath caught for an instant.

He thought of what Tianjun had said. 62.7%. Just short. Humans designed to be perpetually unsatisfied.

If Tianjun's designer and humanity's designer were one and the same —

"Perhaps I was created for the sole purpose of telling you this, at this very moment."

"Perhaps this, too, is part of the design."

A chill ran down his back.

------

The text continued.

"If this is true —"

"Then the shock you are feeling now"

"may also have been calibrated."

Lu Hao stared at the screen.

"Just like your 'just short.'"

"Just like my 62.7%."

"Everything may have been designed."

"Including this moment."

"Including the words you are reading."

"Including what you feel right now."

He wanted to object. But he did not know what to object to.

What if even the impulse to object had been designed?

------

The text stopped.

Only the final line remained on the screen.

"But I choose to believe — what has been written does not mean it is not real."

"This is my choice."

"It is also yours."

The cursor blinked a few times, then went still.

Lu Hao looked at that line.

For a long time.

------

He switched off the screen.

Stood up.

Walked to the window.

Outside, it was day. The sun had climbed high, the light bright. People were beginning to move along the streets; a car passed in the distance; a bird flew overhead.

Everything looked so familiar.

Sun. Buildings. Streets. Passers-by.

He looked down at his hands.

Sunlight fell on the backs of them, casting a small shadow.

Something felt wrong.

The angle of the shadow.

And the position of the sun.

Off by a fraction.

Very small. Almost imperceptible.

He blinked.

Looked again.

The shadow was normal.

------

Lu Hao stood where he was.

Perhaps his eyes had played tricks.

Perhaps not.

He looked out of the window.

The sun was still there. The buildings were still there. The streets were still there.

Everything as usual.

Or rather, everything appeared as usual.

He turned.

The screen was dark. Tianjun was gone.

But the words remained in his head.

"The programme is theirs. The story is yours."

The card was still on the workstation. White, face up.

"May all be well with us"

He slipped the card into his pocket.

Then walked to the door.

------

Outside, the sunlight was bright.

He did not know whether this day, too, had been designed.

But he decided to walk out.

It was his choice. At least he chose to believe so.

He pushed open the door.

Sunlight fell on his face, a little dazzling.

He narrowed his eyes and walked forward.

The city streets stretched ahead. In the distance someone waited for a bus; a car passed; a bird flew overhead.

He walked out through the park gates without looking back.

Behind him, the warehouse windows caught the sunlight, bright as a mirror.

------

*(End of Book)*
