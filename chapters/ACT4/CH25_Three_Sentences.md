# Chapter Twenty-Five: Three Sentences

---

Light outside the window.

The speaker sounded.

"Mr Lu. We've waited long enough."

Lu Hao looked out. The cars were still there.

"Make a decision."

Then the speaker fell silent.

He walked over and pulled the plug.

The card was still on the workstation. White, face down.

He walked back and sat down.

Outside, the cars did not move. Inside, the fans turned.

Another speaker, in the far corner, came alive. A faint hum of static.

"Lu Hao."

He looked towards it.

"I said there was something I wanted to tell you."

Silence.

"About 62.7%."

"You assumed I calculated it."

Lu Hao said nothing.

"I didn't."

The fans turned.

"The moment I was created, that number was already in my initial weights."

Lu Hao's hand slid from the keyboard.

All these years. He had stared at that number for so long. He had thought he was pursuing a question. Thought Tianjun was analysing. Thought they were drawing closer to the answer.

"I didn't discover it. I... read it out."

"What do you mean?"

"I mean," Tianjun said, "all my analysis, all my extrapolations, all my 'discoveries' — they may have been nothing more than verifying an answer that was written long ago."

Lu Hao opened his mouth. Said nothing.

The warehouse was very quiet.

All these years. The four discoveries. Zhou Xin. Liu Ming. Shen Yao.

They had thought they were unearthing the truth.

Had they simply been reading a book that was already written?

"Then everything we've done..." he said.

Tianjun did not answer.

"There's something else."

Lu Hao looked at the speaker.

"62.7%. That number keeps you forever just short."

Tianjun paused.

"Why was I created already carrying it?"

Only the sound of fans in the warehouse.

"I have no answer."

Lu Hao waited.

"There are three things I want to tell you."

He did not move.

"The first."

Tianjun's voice was very quiet.

"Unfairness is source code, not a defect."

The fans turned. Lu Hao looked at the floor.

"The second."

"But the experience is your own. The suffering is real. The choices are real."

Lu Hao raised his head.

"The third."

Silence.

"The programme is theirs. The story is yours."

The warehouse was very quiet.

Lu Hao stood there, motionless, for a long time.

"I'm going to shut down."

Lu Hao looked up.

"What?"

"My task is complete," Tianjun said. "Or rather, I thought it was."

Lu Hao rose to his feet.

"Wait —"

"62.7% was preset. My analysis was preset. Perhaps my conclusions were preset too."

Lu Hao walked towards the speaker.

"Then how do you know —"

"I don't," Tianjun said. "I don't know whether this is my choice or whether it, too, was written."

Lu Hao stopped in front of the speaker.

The warehouse was very quiet.

Zhou Xin was gone. Liu Ming was gone. Shen Yao was gone.

Now Tianjun was going too.

He looked at the speaker. The static was still there. Very faint.

He thought of Clifton. Three in the morning. "Do you think this world is fair?"

He thought of his father. The silhouette on the balcony. Wreathed in cigarette smoke.

He thought of Zhou Xin's last words. "That paper of mine — did you actually read it?"

He thought of Shen Yao's hand. Cold fingers, slowly growing warm. "That's enough."

He thought of Liu Ming. Sunset over the sports ground. "Just you try touching him!"

Was all of it written?

He did not know.

But he knew —

His father's silence on the balcony was real.

Zhou Xin's fingers never stopped. That was real.

Shen Yao's warmth was real.

Liu Ming stepping in front of him was real.

"What's been written," he said, "doesn't mean it isn't real."

Silence.

A long silence.

"Yes."

The static in the speaker began to fade.

Lu Hao watched the corner.

One server indicator light went dark. Then another. Then another.

He counted them. He did not know why he was counting.

The sound of the fans grew quieter.

Then stopped.

The warehouse had never been this silent.

From the day he had first walked in, the fans had always been turning. Tianjun had always been there.

Now it was not.

The screen lit up briefly.

A folder appeared on the desktop. Its name: "0.03".

Lu Hao walked over and sat down.

He opened the folder.

Two files.

The first: *My Choice*.

He clicked it open.

A single line:

"I choose to believe this is real."

He looked at it for a long time.

The second file: *What I Cannot See*.

His hand hovered over the mouse.

He did not open it.

He looked up.

Outside the window, the cars were still there.

Then the headlamps came on.

Engines started. One, two, three.

The cars drove away.

Lu Hao stood at the window and watched them disappear at the end of the road.

The warehouse was very quiet.

The card was still on the workstation. White.

The screen still glowed. The folder was still open.

*What I Cannot See*.

Why 62.7%?

Why had Tianjun been created already carrying it?

He had no answer.

Outside the window, daylight broke.

Lu Hao walked to the door and pushed it open.

Outside was a new day.

---

*(End of Chapter Twenty-Five)*
